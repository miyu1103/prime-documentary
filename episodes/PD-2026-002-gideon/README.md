## Central question

If you can't afford a lawyer in America, do you have the right to have one appointed —
and who actually pays for that promise?

## The case

Gideon v. Wainwright, 372 U.S. 335 (1963). Unanimous 9–0.
Author: Justice Hugo Black.
Overruled: Betts v. Brady (1942).

Clarence Earl Gideon — convicted in 1961 without a lawyer — sent a handwritten
petition to the Supreme Court. The Court ruled the Sixth Amendment right to counsel
applies to state felony trials via the Fourteenth Amendment. Gideon was retried with
an appointed lawyer (Abe Fortas) and acquitted.

## Series thread

Miranda (ep 1) warned suspects of their rights. Gideon answers the next question:
what if you can't afford the lawyer Miranda mentioned?
