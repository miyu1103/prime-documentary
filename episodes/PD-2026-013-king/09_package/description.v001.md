In Maryland v. King, the Supreme Court asked whether police may take a DNA swab from a person who has been arrested but not yet convicted.

Alonzo King was arrested on an assault charge. The cheek swab came later during booking, and the DNA database hit connected him to a separate unsolved rape case. This episode keeps that sequence clear: the swab was not the reason for the arrest.

The majority treated the swab as part of identification, like fingerprints and photographs. The dissent saw something more dangerous: a search of people who had merely been arrested. One vote decided the line.

This is an educational documentary, not legal advice.
