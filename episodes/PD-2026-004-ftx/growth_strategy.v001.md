# PD-2026-004-ftx — Growth Strategy (owner-directed, 2026-06-18)

Owner goal for this episode: maximize **views, retention, rewatchability, shares, likes, and
comments** — while staying advertiser-safe and BAN-safe (north star). Every craft choice below
serves one of those six levers. Aligns with `decisions/0005` (packaging) and `decisions/0006`
(growth levers).

## 1. Thumbnail (CTR) — A/B test, high contrast, one curiosity gap
- **Big number + vanish**: a glowing vault / phone screen reading **`$8,000,000,000`** with the
  number half dissolving into nothing. Bold, legible at small size. No real face.
- **Hooded silhouette** (anonymous, NOT a likeness) + a single line of glowing code, or an empty
  vault door. Villain/mystery vibe.
- Text overlay options (≤4 words, huge): "WHERE DID IT GO?", "$8 BILLION GONE", "IT WAS IN THE CODE".
- Color: dark background, one hot accent (acid green / gold) = scroll-stopping contrast.
- Rule: thumbnail asks a question the title half-answers; the video is the payoff. Never clickbait
  a claim we don't deliver.

## 2. Title (CTR + search) — curiosity gap, concrete, true
- A-side: **"He Promised to Give It All Away. Then $8 Billion Vanished."**
- B-side: **"The Hidden Line of Code That Stole $8 Billion"**
- C-side (search-lean): **"How FTX Really Collapsed — And Where the Money Went"**
- Keep "FTX" and "$8 billion" present for searchability. Question/promise framing for clicks.

## 3. Retention (watch time) — front-load, escalate, open loops
- **Cold open (first 5–10s):** "Your money is safe here. That's what 1 million people were told.
  This is where it actually went." Hard hook, no slow logo intro before the hook.
- **Open loops:** tease "one line of code" early, pay it off at the midpoint; tease "the week it all
  collapsed" and pay off near the end.
- **Escalation structure:** golden boy → the promise → the hidden door → where the money went →
  the bank run → the verdict. Each chapter ends on a mini-cliffhanger.
- **Pace:** cut every 3–6s, motion on every shot (no static slideshow — same QC gate as ep3),
  on-screen numbers animate up. Pattern-interrupts at ~every 30–45s.
- **No dead air at the start; save the disclaimer to a quick lower-third, not a cold lecture.**

## 4. Rewatchability — layered, satisfying, "catch it the second time"
- Clean timeline graphic the viewer wants to re-scan; a couple of blink-and-miss detail callouts.
- Tight, self-contained chapters so people re-watch the "backdoor explained" segment specifically.
- A crisp recap card at the end that makes a re-skim rewarding.

## 5. Shareability — one "holy crap" takeaway + a portable lesson
- The shareable fact: **a single setting in the code — `allow_negative` — let his own firm spend
  customers' deposits.** Easy to repeat in a text to a friend.
- Portable lesson: **"Not your keys, not your coins"** — an exchange holding your money is a black
  box. Relatable to anyone, even non-crypto viewers.
- A 30–45s self-contained mid-section engineered to work as a clip/Short for cross-posting.

## 6. Likes — earn it with payoff + one explicit, well-timed ask
- Deliver a satisfying justice arc (convicted on all 7 counts, 25 years) = emotionally rewarding.
- Single, well-placed like-prompt right after the most satisfying reveal (the verdict), not at the
  cold open. Tie it to value: "If this finally made FTX make sense, hit like."

## 7. Comments — end on a genuine debate question + invite stories
- Closing question (pinned comment too): **"25 years — too much, too little, or just right?"**
- Secondary debate hook: **"Effective altruism: was he a true believer or was it always a cover?"**
- Invite: "Did you (or someone you know) have money on FTX? What happened?"
- These are real, non-divisive-enough-to-be-unsafe debates → high comment volume, repeat visits.

## Safety guardrails (non-negotiable, north star)
- State only adjudicated facts; attribute trial claims ("a co-founder testified…", "prosecutors
  said…"). He was **convicted** — that we state plainly.
- No real-person likeness (anonymous silhouettes only); no deepfakes.
- "Not financial or legal advice" + AI-generated-visuals disclosure.
- Don't imply crimes by people who weren't convicted; describe non-defendants by role where possible.
- Symbolic reconstruction only, never presented as authentic footage.
