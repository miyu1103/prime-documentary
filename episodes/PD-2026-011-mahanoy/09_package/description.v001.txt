A teenager posted an angry, profane Snapchat off campus, on a weekend, from her own phone. The school punished her for it. In 2021, the Supreme Court had to answer a question every student and parent now lives with: where does a school's power over your speech end?

This is Mahanoy Area School District v. B.L. (2021), the case about off-campus student speech, the old Tinker rule, and why the phone made the "schoolhouse gate" much harder to find.
