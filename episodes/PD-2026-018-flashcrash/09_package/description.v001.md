On May 6, 2010, nearly $1 trillion in market value briefly vanished in about 36 minutes. This Prime Documentary follows the Flash Crash, Navinder Singh Sarao, spoofing, high-frequency liquidity, a large E-mini sell order, and the legal aftermath.

Causation note: Sarao pleaded guilty to wire fraud and spoofing. This film does not claim that he single-handedly caused the Flash Crash. It presents the official/legal framing that his conduct contributed to or exacerbated order-book imbalance inside a wider event that also involved large sell pressure, liquidity withdrawal, and market anxiety.

Visuals are symbolic reconstructions, AI-generated stills, licensed factory atmosphere, and Remotion motion graphics. No real-person likeness of Sarao or family members is used.
