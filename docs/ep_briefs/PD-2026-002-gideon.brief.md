## The true story (verify against sources)
Clarence Earl Gideon — a poor drifter — was charged with breaking into a Florida pool hall (1961),
denied a court-appointed lawyer, defended himself, and was convicted. From his prison cell he
**handwrote a petition (in pencil)** to the U.S. Supreme Court. The Court took it. **Gideon v.
Wainwright, 372 U.S. 335 (1963)** — unanimous (Justice Hugo Black) — established the right to
appointed counsel in state felony cases (via the 14th Amendment). Retried **with** a lawyer, Gideon
was **acquitted**. The underdog beat the system with a pencil.

## Thesis (the hidden system)
A constitutional right can exist on paper and still not reach you — until someone forces the system
to honor it. Gideon's pencil letter turned "you may hire a lawyer" into "the state must give you
one." (Sets up EP3: having a lawyer ≠ a real defense.)
